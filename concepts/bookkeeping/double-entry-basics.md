# Double-entry bookkeeping basics

In Belgium a company always keeps **double-entry** bookkeeping (some small sole traders may opt for simplified , see [[eenmanszaak-vs-vennootschap]]). Every transaction is booked **twice**: a **debit** (where the value goes) and a **credit** (where it comes from), so debit always equals credit and the books stay in balance.

## The flow

- **Journals / dagboeken** record entries chronologically , typically a purchases, sales, financial, and miscellaneous journal. [search]
- The **general ledger (grootboek)** holds every account (the [[chart-of-accounts-mar|MAR accounts]]); journal entries post to it. [search]
- A **trial balance (proef- en saldibalans)** proves debit = credit. [search]

## The two reports

- **Balance sheet (balans):** a snapshot , **assets = liabilities + equity**. Holds what the company owns and owes (MAR classes 1-5).
- **Profit & loss (resultatenrekening):** a period view , **income minus costs** (MAR classes 6-7), closed against equity.
- Costs/income live only in the P&L; assets/liabilities only on the balance sheet. Most transactions touch one of each.

At year-end these become the **annual accounts** (balance sheet + P&L + notes), filed at the NBB ([[annual-accounts-nbb]]).
