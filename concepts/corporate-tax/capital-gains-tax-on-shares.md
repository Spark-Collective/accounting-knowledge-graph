# Capital gains tax on shares (solidarity contribution)

A new **solidarity contribution (~10%)** on **future realized capital gains on financial assets** (shares, etc.). It is a personal-level tax that matters when a director sells a participation. The treatment splits by the size of the holding.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **Small participation (< 20%):** the gain is taxed at the **~10%** solidarity contribution. [astro]
- **Large participation (>= 20%):** a graduated regime , **up to EUR 1 million of gain is fully exempt**, then a low rate kicks in (e.g. ~1.25% on the EUR 1-2.5 million band), rising in higher bands. [astro]

> **confidence: low + verify_live.** This is a new measure and the thresholds/rates/bands are evolving. **Confirm the current rules with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be) or the accountant** before any share sale , do not act on these figures alone.

## Why it matters

It changes the calculus on selling a (management) company or a participation, alongside the [[vvpr-bis-dividends|dividend]] and [[liquidation-reserve|liquidation-reserve]] routes for getting value out of a BV , so it belongs in the same payout/exit planning the agent supports.
