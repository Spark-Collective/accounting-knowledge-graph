# Liquidation reserve (liquidatiereserve)

A KMO payout-planning mechanism, alternative to a direct [[vvpr-bis-dividends|dividend]]: the company books part of its **taxed profit** to a separate liquidation reserve, paying a small anticipative tax now, in exchange for a low withholding when the reserve is later distributed.

## The mechanism (verify_live)

- Set aside taxed profit + pay **~10% anticipative corporate tax** up front. [search]
- After a **waiting period**, distribute at a **reduced withholding** (historically 5% after 5 years, i.e. ~15% total); distributing **earlier** costs more (~20%). At **liquidation**, no extra withholding regardless of the wait. [search]

## The 2025 reform (Paasakkoord) , NOT yet final

The Easter agreement **shortens the waiting period and slightly raises the later rate** , the aim is to let owners pay out sooner at a modestly higher cost. **These measures still need parliamentary approval and may change.** [search]

> **confidence: low + verify_live.** Confirm the current waiting period, the anticipative rate, and the distribution withholding with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be) or the accountant before acting , the numbers are in flux.

## Why it matters

Liquidation reserve vs [[vvpr-bis-dividends|VVPR-bis]] vs [[director-remuneration|salary]] is the core payout decision for a profitable one-person BV; the 2025 changes shift the trade-off, so the agent must use live figures.
