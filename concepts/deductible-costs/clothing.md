# Work clothing (beroepskledij)

Only **specific professional clothing** is deductible , the tax authority is strict here, so this is a frequent rejection.

## The rule

- **Specific professional clothing: 100% deductible.** Items specific to the profession, **barely wearable privately**, or **required by regulation** , safety clothing, overalls, work shoes, helmets, aprons. [search]
- **Ordinary clothing: not deductible.** Suits, normal shoes, anything also worn privately , rejected, even with a company logo if it has no genuine professional character. [search]
- **VAT** on specific professional clothing is deductible with a valid invoice in the company's name ([[vat-deduction-conditions]]). [search]
- **Logo clothing** may sometimes count as a publicity cost (cf. [[sponsoring]]); **non-specific** clothing bought via the company can be taxed as a **benefit in kind** ([[benefits-in-kind-vaa]]) on the recipient. [search]

## Booking

Book specific work clothing to a class-6 cost account; flag ordinary clothing as non-deductible in [[expense-categorization]] (a disallowed expense, like [[fines]]).
