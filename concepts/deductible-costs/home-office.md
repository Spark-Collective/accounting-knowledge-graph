# Home-office allowance (bureauvergoeding)

A company can pay its director a **fixed monthly office allowance** for structurally working from home , **tax-free and RSZ-free** , covering the cost of a home workspace. Like the [[per-diem-domestic-travel|per diem]], it is a clean way to move private-overlap costs into the company without a benefit.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **Max ~EUR 160.99/month** (from 1 March 2026, indexed). [search]
- Covers: use of a workspace in the private home, small office material, maintenance/cleaning, electricity/water/heating, insurance, onroerende voorheffing. [search]
- **Conditions:** the person **regularly and structurally** works from home, and the company does **not** otherwise cover these office costs. Without structural home work, the forfait does not apply (reimbursement is then only untaxed if the company proves the costs are its own and actually spent). [search]
- **Only via a company:** a self-employed person **without** a company ([[eenmanszaak-vs-vennootschap|eenmanszaak]]) cannot use this forfait. [search]

> The amount is indexed (changed 1 March 2026). **Confirm the current ceiling** (RSZ instructions / Circulaire 2021/C/20) before paying; over-paying without proof makes the excess taxable.

## Why it matters

A recurring, fully untaxed reimbursement for a one-person BV run from home , the agent applies the current ceiling monthly. Distinct from the car-side [[benefits-in-kind-vaa|VAA]].
