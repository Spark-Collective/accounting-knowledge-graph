# Restaurant and business meal costs

Restaurant and meal costs (meals + drinks served in an eatery, business dinners and lunches, event catering) are deductible only when the meal has a **business character** , it must relate to earning or keeping taxable income, e.g. a meal with a client, prospect, supplier or partner. The type of venue (restaurant vs sandwich shop) does not change the qualification.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **Income-tax deductibility: ~69%.** Even a meal that is 100% business is only ~69% deductible , tax is still paid on the remaining ~31%. [astro]
- **VAT on restaurant costs is, in principle, NOT deductible** , even with a VAT receipt. [astro]
- A **lunch during work hours** is deductible only with a clear professional purpose (lunch with a client/supplier/colleague to discuss business). A solo meal is not deductible. [astro]
- **Proof:** note on the receipt **who** you ate with and **why** ("lunch with client X re project Y"). This substantiates the business character for a control. [astro]

> Confirm the current percentage with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be) before applying to a real booking.

## Booking

Book to the dedicated restaurant-cost account in class 6 (see [[chart-of-accounts-mar]]). The non-deductible fraction is a **tax add-back** in the corporate-tax computation ([[year-end]], planned), not a bookkeeping exclusion , book the full cost. The non-deductible VAT is part of the cost.
