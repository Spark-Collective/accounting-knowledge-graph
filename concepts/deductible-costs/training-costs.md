# Training costs and the KMO-portefeuille

Professional training that improves the business is a deductible cost; on top of that, a company with a Flemish establishment can recover part of it through the **KMO-portefeuille**, a Flemish-government subsidy for SME training and advice.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **Deductibility:** genuinely professional training is a deductible cost (class-6, see [[chart-of-accounts-mar]]); apply the [[expense-categorization|professional-character test]].
- **KMO-portefeuille subsidy** (Flanders): a small enterprise gets **~30%** back, a medium enterprise **~20%**, each up to **~EUR 7,500/year**. [astro]
- **Conditions:** an SME/self-employed with an establishment **in Flanders**, using a **recognized provider**, for training that improves business operations. [astro]

> Subsidy rates, caps, and eligibility change. **Confirm at the current KMO-portefeuille source** (Flemish government) before relying on them. The subsidy is regional (Flanders); other regions differ.

## Booking

Book the full training cost as an expense; the subsidy received is income/an offset , do not net it silently. Size eligibility ties to [[company-size-criteria]].
