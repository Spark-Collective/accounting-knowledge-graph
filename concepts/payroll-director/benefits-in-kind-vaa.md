# Benefits in kind (Voordeel van Alle Aard, VAA)

A VAA is a benefit the company gives the director (or an employee) for private use , a company car, free housing, a phone/internet subscription, an interest-free loan, etc. It is added to the recipient's **taxable professional income**. The tax authority values it at either its **real value** or a **forfait** (fixed formula); the real value is often lower and so preferable.

## Common forfaits (verify_live)

- **Company car:** a forfait on the vehicle's **catalogue price and CO2 emissions**; there is an indexed annual minimum (the 2026 minimum is indexed, confirm). Interacts with [[car-and-mobility|car-cost deductibility]]. [search]
- **Smartphone:** ~**EUR 36/year**; **internet:** ~**EUR 60/year** (standard forfaits). [search]
- **Free housing (director):** a higher formula of **3.8 x the indexed cadastral income** (the old natural-vs-legal-person distinction was ruled unconstitutional). [search]
- **Reimbursed to the company -> not taxed** (the director pays the benefit back, no VAA). [search]

> Confirm all forfait figures with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be) before applying , several are indexed yearly.

## Why it matters

VAA is part of the director's remuneration package, so it ties into [[director-remuneration|salary planning]]: in 2026 a director's total remuneration may consist of **at most 20% forfait VAA**, which matters for the EUR 45,000 minimum that unlocks the [[corporate-tax-basics|20% corporate rate]]. The agent must value each benefit and book the matching VAA.
