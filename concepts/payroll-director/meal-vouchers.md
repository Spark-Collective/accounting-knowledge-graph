# Meal vouchers (maaltijdcheques)

A director of a BV / CommV / management company is both employer and employee, so can grant themselves **meal vouchers** as part of remuneration , exempt from social security and not taxable income if the statutory conditions hold. A popular net-income optimizer.

## The rule (verify_live)

- **Value (since 1 Jan 2026): max EUR 10 per day actually worked**, split into an **employer part (max ~EUR 8.91)** and an **employee part (min ~EUR 1.09)**. [search]
- **Exempt from RSZ + income tax** for the recipient when the conditions hold: one voucher per day actually worked, electronic, not exchangeable for cash. [search]
- **Company deductibility is limited:** only a **small fixed amount per voucher (historically ~EUR 2.00)** is deductible; the rest is a disallowed expense (a year-end add-back, cf. [[expense-categorization]]). [search]
- Providers (Monizze, Edenred, Pluxee) offer the same statutory product.

> The EUR 10 / EUR 8.91 / EUR 2 figures are indexed/changeable. **Confirm current amounts with [FOD Financiën](https://financien.belgium.be) / RSZ.**

## Why it matters

One of the cleanest ways to raise the director's net without extra contributions , part of [[director-remuneration|pay planning]] alongside salary, dividend, the [[per-diem-domestic-travel|per diem]], and the [[home-office|home-office allowance]].
