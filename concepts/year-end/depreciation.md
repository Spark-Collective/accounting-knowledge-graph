# Depreciation (afschrijvingen) , practice

The working procedure for spreading a fixed asset's cost over its life. The accounting principles are grounded in the [[depreciation-advies|CBN advies]] and the [[valuation-rules-advies|valuation frame]]; this page is how the agent applies them.

## In the books

- Set a **depreciation plan** over the asset's **economic useful life** (e.g. laptop 3 years, machinery longer), method **linear** (most common) or degressive.
- Book the annual charge to **account 630** (afschrijvingen, a class-6 cost) against the **accumulated depreciation** on the class-2 asset (see [[chart-of-accounts-mar]]).
- A **pro-rata** charge often applies in the year of acquisition.
- Add **exceptional depreciation** if the carrying value exceeds the asset's usage value ([[depreciation-advies|advies]]).

## Book vs tax

- The **book** depreciation follows the plan; the **tax** rules can differ (e.g. first-year limits, accepted accelerated systems). Track both.
- The [[investment-deduction]] is a **separate** tax benefit on top of depreciation, not a substitute.
- Small items below a threshold may be expensed at once (verify the current threshold).

## Why it matters

Depreciation is the main recurring year-end entry that shapes the result, and it interacts with the [[corporate-tax-basics|tax base]]. The agent maintains the asset register and posts the annual charge before the [[annual-accounts-nbb|annual accounts]].
