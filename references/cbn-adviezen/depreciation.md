# CBN advies , depreciation (afschrijvingen)

A `references/cbn-adviezen/` page: the CBN/CNC accounting principles for depreciating fixed assets, which ground the practice in [[chart-of-accounts-mar|the books]] and at [[annual-accounts-nbb|year-end]].

## What the advies says

- Fixed assets are depreciated on a **plan set by the governing body** to spread the acquisition cost over the asset's **probable useful life for the company**. [cbn]
- The regime may be **linear**, **degressive**, or tied to **objective criteria** (usage intensity, production volume) , all acceptable for the annual accounts if they match either the spread over useful life or a tax-accepted accelerated system. [cbn]
- Useful life is the **economic** life, not the legal life. [cbn]
- **Supplementary (exceptional) depreciation** is required when an asset's carrying value exceeds its **usage value** due to technical or economic/technological obsolescence. [cbn]
- A sale/replacement value above carrying value does **not** justify stopping the depreciation plan. [cbn]
- **Book vs tax depreciation** can differ (tax: KB WIB 92 art. 36-38 for degressive). [cbn]

> The accounting principle is stable; the **tax** depreciation rules and the [[investment-deduction]] change , verify those live.
