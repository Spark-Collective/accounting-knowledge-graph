# CBN advies , distribution test (net-asset + liquidity)

A `references/cbn-adviezen/` page: under the WVV, a BV may only distribute profit (dividend, [[vvpr-bis-dividends|VVPR-bis]], [[liquidation-reserve|liquidation reserve]]) after passing a **double test**. The CBN advies explains both.

## What the advies says

### 1. Net-asset test (art. 5:142 WVV for the BV)

- After the distribution, the company's **net asset may not become (or threaten to become) negative**. [cbn]
- **Net asset** = total assets minus provisions, debts, unamortized formation/expansion costs, and R&D costs; statutorily **unavailable contributions** must also be respected. [cbn]
- If the net asset would go negative, the **alarm-bell procedure** applies (art. 5:153 WVV). [cbn]

### 2. Liquidity test

- The governing body must judge that, after the distribution, the company can **pay its due debts for at least 12 months**. [cbn]
- It is a **forward-looking** assessment of the liquidity position, accounting for near-certain events in that period. [cbn]
- It is done **after the distribution decision but before the actual payment** to beneficiaries. [cbn]

## Grounds

The payout decisions in [[vvpr-bis-dividends]], [[liquidation-reserve]], and [[director-remuneration]] , a distribution is only legal if both tests pass.
